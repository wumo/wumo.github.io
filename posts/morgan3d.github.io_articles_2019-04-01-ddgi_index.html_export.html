      <span class="md"><p><title>Dynamic Diffuse Global Illumination</title><div class="title"> Dynamic Diffuse Global Illumination </div>

<div class="subtitle"> Part 1 </div>
<div class="subtitle"> 2019 April 1 </div>
<div class="subtitle"> Updated May 22 </div>
<div class="afterTitles"></div>

</p><p>

Dynamic Diffuse Global Illumination (DDGI) is a technique for computing
diffuse lighting with ray tracing to create changing, realistic rendering
for games. It allows developers to extend their existing light probe
tools, knowledge, and experience with ray tracing to eliminate bake
times and avoid light leaking.

</p><p>

Hardware-accelerated programmable ray tracing is now accessable
through DXR, VulkanRT, OptiX, Unreal Engine, and Unity. This enables
many ways to compute global illumination. DDGI is a new strategy for
lighting that expands developers' options.

</p><p>

<p>


Here's an example of the image quality that DDGI provides. These
images were rendered at 1080p on GeForce RTX 2080 Ti at 90 fps.

</p><p>

DDGI contributed 1 ms/frame to the render cost. That includes the
amortized bounding volume hierarchy (BVH) update, ray cast, ray shade,
and gathering in screen space during deferred shading.

</p><p>

DDGI is about diffuse lighting, but it also accelerates very rough and
second-bounce <em class="underscore">glossy</em> global illumination. Moreover, this article
explains how to combine the ray trace operations for smooth glossy
illumination with DDGI to improve the ray tracing throughput of
each. The sample glossy GI cost an additional 2 ms/frame in this
implementation, where it is configured for highest image quality.

<a class="target" name="advantages">&nbsp;</a><a class="target" name="advantages">&nbsp;</a><a class="target" name="toc2">&nbsp;</a><h1>Advantages</h1>
<p>


DDGI is a good fit for:

</p><p>

<ul>
<li class="minus"><em class="underscore">Indoor-outdoor and indoor</em>: DDGI avoids <em class="asterisk">light leaks</em>
</li>
<li class="minus"><em class="underscore">Game runtimes</em>: DDGI guarantees performance, <em class="asterisk">scales</em> across GPUs and resolutions up to 4k, and has <em class="asterisk">no noise</em>
</li>
<li class="minus"><em class="underscore">Game production</em>: DDGI <em class="asterisk">avoids baking time</em> and per-probe tuning and accelerates your existing light probe workflow and expertise
</li>
<li class="minus"><em class="underscore">Any light type</em>: DDGI automatically works with point, line, and area lights, skybox lighting, and emissive objects
</li>
<li class="minus"><em class="underscore">Engines</em>: DDGI upgrades existing <em class="asterisk">light probe</em> engine data paths and tools
</li>
<li class="minus"><em class="underscore">Content creation</em>: DDGI works with <em class="asterisk">fully dynamic</em> scenes and does not require manual intervention
</li>
<li class="minus"><em class="underscore">Scalability</em>: <em class="asterisk">One code path</em> gives a smooth spectrum from baked light probes that avoid leaks 
  on legacy platforms, through slowly updating runtime GI on budget GPUs, all of the way up to instant dynamic GI on enthusiast GPUs.
  Unlock the power of high end PCs while still supporting a broad base.</li></ul>

</p>
<a class="target" name="costs">&nbsp;</a><a class="target" name="costs">&nbsp;</a><a class="target" name="toc3">&nbsp;</a><h1>Costs</h1>
<p>


The major costs and limitations of DDGI are:

</p><p>

<ul>
<li class="minus">5 MB GPU RAM per cascade, peak <em class="asterisk">20 MB</em> for all cascades and intermediates
</li>
<li class="minus"><em class="asterisk">1-2 ms</em> per frame for best performance in fixed-time mode, or <em class="asterisk">1-2 Mrays</em> per frame for ultra, fixed-quality mode (on 2080 Ti those are the same)
</li>
<li class="minus">Overhead is minimized when also using ray tracing for other effects, such as glossy and shadow rays
</li>
<li class="minus">DDGI should be introduced early in look development and production. 
  GI changes lighting substantially, and requires more physically-plausible
  geometry and tuning than direct light, and offers new gameplay opportunities. 
</li>
<li class="minus"><em class="asterisk">Lighting flows slower</em> on low-end GPUs (world-space lag, no screen-space ghosting)
</li>
<li class="minus">Shadowmap-like <em class="asterisk">bias parameter</em> must be tuned to scene scale
</li>
<li class="minus">Cannot prevent leaks from zero-thickness/<em class="asterisk">single-sided</em> walls
</li>
<li class="minus">Must be paired with a <em class="asterisk">separate glossy global illumination</em> solution, such as screen-space ray tracing, 
  denoised geometric ray tracing, or environment probes</li></ul>

</p>
